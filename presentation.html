Neuroscience Foundation

Infant cries are generated by the Periaqueductal Gray (PAG) region of the midbrain, producing distinct phonation modes (modal voice, falsetto, fry) depending on the underlying physiological state. The Dunstan Baby Language framework identifies five reflexive cry sounds mapped to basic needs, enabling acoustic classification based on fundamental frequency, formant structure, and spectral characteristics.

Why CQCC?

Unlike MFCC which uses a uniform filterbank, Constant-Q Cepstral Coefficients use a geometrically-spaced filterbank that provides variable time-frequency resolution — higher frequency resolution at low frequencies (capturing fundamental frequency and formant structure) and higher temporal resolution at high frequencies (capturing transient cry onsets). This mirrors the human auditory system and is particularly suited for infant cry analysis where both F0 patterns and burst dynamics carry diagnostic information.

Analysis

Confusion Matrix Analysis

Belly Pain vs. Discomfort

These two classes share overlapping Dunstan reflexes (Eairh/Heh) and similar abdominal tension patterns, leading to occasional misclassification. Both involve physical discomfort with similar phonation modes.

Burping: Hardest Class

With only 8 original samples (fewest in corpus), burping required the most aggressive augmentation. Despite this, XGBoost achieves 0.97 F1 through effective feature engineering and balanced sample weights.

Hunger: Most Distinct

Hunger cries show the most distinctive acoustic signature with consistent Neh reflex patterns, yielding 0.99 recall. The suction-related tongue movement creates a unique spectral fingerprint.

Tiredness: Perfect Precision

XGBoost achieves 1.00 precision on tiredness cries. The yawn-like Owh reflex creates a distinct low-frequency pattern that is never confused with other classes, though some tiredness samples are missed (0.93 recall).

Insights

Key Findings

CQCC features outperform MFCC-only baselines

Variable time-frequency resolution from the Constant-Q Transform captures both F0 patterns (low-freq resolution) and cry onset transients (high-freq temporal resolution), providing complementary information to standard MFCCs.

SMOTE resolves class imbalance

SVM F1-score improved from 0.25 to 0.92 after applying Synthetic Minority Oversampling Technique, demonstrating that imbalanced training data was the primary bottleneck rather than model capacity.

XGBoost achieves 96.5% accuracy

Gradient boosting with regularization provides the best single-model performance, effectively learning complex decision boundaries in the 411-dimensional feature space with built-in feature selection via importance scores.

Ensemble achieves highest AUC-ROC (0.994)

Stacking SVM, Random Forest, and XGBoost with a Logistic Regression meta-learner yields near-perfect class separability, suggesting the models capture complementary patterns in the feature space.

Roadmap

Phase 2 & 3 Preview

Building on Phase 1's strong classical ML baseline, the next phases introduce deep learning for end-to-end representation learning.

Phase 2 CNN + LSTM on Spectrograms

Convert audio to Mel spectrogram images and train a CNN-LSTM hybrid architecture. The CNN learns spatial patterns (formant structure, harmonic bands) while the LSTM captures temporal dynamics (cry onset, intensity contour, breathing patterns). Target: surpass 96.5% accuracy.

Phase 2 Wav2Vec 2.0 Transfer Learning

Fine-tune a pretrained Wav2Vec 2.0 self-supervised model on infant cry data. Leverages representations learned from 960h of speech data (LibriSpeech), adapting high-level audio understanding to the cry classification domain. Expected to learn nuanced acoustic features that hand-crafted features miss.

Phase 3 Real-Time Mobile Inference

Deploy the best model via ONNX/TFLite for real-time inference on mobile devices. Build a prototype app that records infant cries and provides instant classification with confidence scores, enabling caregivers to respond more effectively to their infant's needs.
